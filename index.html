What Is Coronavirus ?

Coronaviruses are a family of viruses that can cause illnesses in both animals and humans. The name "coronavirus" is derived from the Latin corona, meaning crown or halo, which refers to the characteristic appearance of the virus particles. Under a microscope, these viruses exhibit a fringe of large, bulbous surface projections creating an image reminiscent of a royal crown.

Among the various types of coronaviruses, some cause mild respiratory infections in humans, similar to the common cold. However, more severe strains have emerged, such as the notorious Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus (SARS-CoV) and Middle East Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus (MERS-CoV), both of which have caused significant outbreaks with considerable mortality rates. The most recent addition to this list is the novel coronavirus, SARS-CoV-2, identified in late 2019 as the causative agent of the COVID-19 pandemic.
